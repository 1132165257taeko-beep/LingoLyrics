# Annotation Guidelines

Write annotations for Minnan lyric study in Simplified Chinese, aimed at native Mandarin speakers.

## Style

- Keep each note short, objective, and useful: usually one sentence, at most two.
- Use numbered markers: `①`, `②`, `③`, etc.
- Put each numbered annotation on its own line.
- Use this format: `①词或短语（闽拼）：词性或功能，简短解释；必要时补充与普通话的差异。`
- Prefer concrete language points over broad song interpretation.
- Do not annotate just to fill space. A line may have no annotations.

## Annotate

- Minnan-specific words that a Mandarin speaker cannot infer reliably.
- False friends: characters that look Mandarin-like but have different meaning, usage, or tone in Minnan.
- Colloquial particles, sentence-final particles, aspect markers, negation, comparison, question forms, and common contractions.
- Idioms, fixed phrases, reduplication, kinship terms, religious/cultural references, or local place/culture terms.
- Character choices that are phonetic loans, nonstandard, dialect characters, or likely written differently across sources.
- Literary vs vernacular readings when the choice affects comprehension or singing.
- Differences between literal meaning and the supplied Chinese translation when useful.

## Usually Do Not Annotate

- Ordinary words that are transparent from Mandarin and used similarly.
- Proper names unless pronunciation, dialect form, or cultural context matters.
- Every particle by default; annotate particles only when their Minnan function changes interpretation.
- Repeated chorus lines already explained nearby, unless the repetition introduces a new form.
- Very simple interjections such as `啊`, `喔`, `啦`, `Hey`, unless the line depends on their dialect nuance.

## Romanization Notes

- Use Minnan Phonetic Scheme / Bbánlám pìngyīm in notes.
- Include tone numbers when known. For draft output, it is acceptable to omit tone numbers consistently if tones are not reliable.
- If an isolated reading is genuinely uncertain, write `[读音待确认]`; do not add this marker to every generated romanization line.
- Do not switch to POJ or Tailo spellings in notes unless the user explicitly requests that system.

## Example Note Shape

```markdown
①毋（m6）：否定词，相当于普通话“不”，但用法与普通话否定系统不完全一一对应。
②欲（beh4）：常表示“要、想要、将要”，歌词里常有愿望或即将发生的语气。
③阮（gun2）：第一人称复数“我们/咱们”，也可按语境指“我方”；普通话母语者容易误按字面理解。
```
